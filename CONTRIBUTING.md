# Contributing

Thank you for your interest in Information Asymmetry. This is an early-stage research programme, and contributions should help make the theory clearer, more testable, and more reproducible.

## Scope of Contributions

Helpful contributions include:

- theoretical comments;
- experimental proposals;
- mathematical corrections;
- engineering notes;
- diagram improvements;
- reproducibility templates;
- references with verifiable source material.

## Research Standards

Please distinguish clearly between:

- formal claims;
- hypotheses;
- engineering evidence;
- speculative future research;
- implementation notes.

Do not present the hypothesis as proven. Do not present Runtime Governance as proof of the psychological hypothesis.

## Medical and Biological Caution

Do not make medical claims without evidence. Biological and healthcare notes in this repository are future research directions unless supported by validated studies, datasets, protocols, and appropriate expert review.

## Issue Templates in Prose

When opening an issue, please include:

1. **Area** — mathematics, experiments, engineering, references, future research, or documentation.
2. **Claim type** — correction, question, proposal, limitation, replication, or reference.
3. **Evidence** — equations, data, source links, logs, or clear reasoning.
4. **Expected change** — what file or concept should be updated.
5. **Risk** — any uncertainty, unsupported inference, or domain-specific caution.

## Pull Request Guidance

A strong pull request should:

- remain narrowly scoped;
- avoid unsupported claims;
- include citations where references are added;
- preserve readable Markdown formatting;
- update diagrams when concepts change;
- explain whether changes are theoretical, experimental, or engineering-oriented.

## Discussion Norms

Respectful scientific discussion is expected. Critique ideas precisely, state assumptions, and prefer testable proposals over broad assertions.
