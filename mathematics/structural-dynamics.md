# Structural Dynamics

Structural dynamics describes how systems evolve through trajectories, feedback, transitions, and constraints.

Relevant concepts include:

- state variables;
- trajectories through state space;
- transition rules;
- feedback loops;
- stability and instability;
- recovery paths;
- reachable and unreachable regions.

The research programme asks whether these structural descriptions can generate higher-level psychological descriptions and whether the reverse reconstruction loses information.
