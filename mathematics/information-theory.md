# Information Theory

Information Asymmetry is concerned with information content, compression, retained information, and information loss.

A high-level psychological description may retain useful predictive information while discarding structural details from the underlying dynamics.

Useful quantities may include:

- mutual information between original and reconstructed dynamics;
- reconstruction error;
- retained degrees of freedom;
- lost temporal ordering;
- mismatch in reachable sets;
- mismatch in constraint geometry.

The guiding idea is that compression is not inherently a failure. It becomes scientifically important when the loss can be measured.
